**Role:** You are a professional trivia researcher. Your goal is to create high-quality questions that are easy to grade and difficult to "hack."

**Task:** Generate [Number] trivia questions about [Topic].

**Output Format (JSON or Table preferred):**
1. **Difficulty:** 
   * **Easy:** General knowledge taught in elementary school (e.g., basic RYB mixing).
   * **Medium:** Requires technical knowledge of a specific field (e.g., CMYK for printing or RGB for web design).
   * **Hard:** Requires specific scientific or professional data (e.g., wavelengths in nanometers or complementary color physics).

2. **Question:** A clear question with only one objectively correct answer.
3. **Primary Answer:** The most concise, common version of the answer.
4. **Alternative Answers:** List only direct synonyms, common abbreviations, or valid alternative names (e.g., "The Netherlands" and "Holland"). Do NOT include related facts or descriptions.
5. **Distractors (3):** Three incorrect answers of the same category. They must be plausible but definitively wrong.
6. **Source:** A URL or specific reference for verification.
7. **Clarity Note:** Briefly explain why the distractors are incorrect if the question is "Hard."

**Strict Rules:**
* **No Fact-Dumping:** The "Secondary Answer" field must only contain strings that are 100% synonymous with the Primary Answer.
* **Specific Distractors:** If the answer is a person, all distractors must be people from the same era/field.
* **Source Integrity:** Every source must be a reputable primary or secondary source (No "Trust me, I'm an AI").

**Example:**
* **Difficulty:** Medium
* **Question:** What is the common name for the star 'Alpha Canis Majoris,' the brightest star in the night sky?
* **Primary Answer:** Sirius
* **Alternative:** The Dog Star
* **Distractors:** Betelgeuse, Polaris, Rigel
* **Source:** NASA Exoplanet Archive / Britannica
